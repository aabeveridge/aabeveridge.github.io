Teaching

Teaching Portfolio: teaching-portfolio-beveridge.pdf

My research directly informs the courses I design. “Writing through Big Data,” developed and taught in 2015, was the first humanities course at the University of Florida to use the HiperGator supercomputer for research, and students in this course confronted Big Data’s political and ethical concerns head-on (surveillance, privacy, and algorithmic filtering) by collecting social network data and producing their own data-driven arguments. I have taught courses at all levels, including first-year composition and graduate business writing, and I have mentored graduate students in the humanities, communication, and health sciences.

In addition to the teaching and mentoring, my role as a founding member of the Trace Innovations Initiative at UF has allowed me to develop interdisciplinary trainings in text mining (including literary texts), and I have worked with the Marston Science Library at UF to create an ongoing training series in physical computing (Arduino) and the Internet of Things.

Projects

MassMine

I am the co-creator of MassMine. MassMine recieved the largest level II start-up grant available from the NEH in 2015. MassMine collects and curates data from social networks for academic research. MassMine's sources: Twitter, Google Trends, Tumblr, Wikipedia, and any URL. www.massmine.org

Physical Computing and the Internet of Things

Along with Emily Faith, I developed an Arduino programming and prototyping training series for the Marston Science Library at the University of Florida. The training series is open to all students at UF, and provides a basic introduction to making and physical computing with Arduinos. The LibGuides for the training are available here.

Writing Jobs Data Analysis

Inspired by Jim Ridolfo’s work with rhetmap.org, I am curating an archive of job description data and writing studies related analyses from the MLA Jobs Information List (JIL). I am analyzing the data for changes in relative year-to-year word frequencies and correlations related to the terms: “rhetoric,” “composition,” “writing,” and “technical.” All of the data and code are available through my GitHub page. The dataset will contain 10 years of job posting data and analyses once completed. Target goal for completion: Spring 2017.
